1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 4,000 kW sensible — complete Tier 4 facility, single-phase deployment. Sixteen (16) large chilled water AHUs in a
fully redundant 2N configuration: eight active units carry the full 4,000 kW load; eight standby units can assume any combination of
active unit failures within 30 seconds.
Tier 4 dual-CHW-path design: each unit is connected to two fully independent chilled water plants — Plant A and Plant B — each
sized for 100% of total campus cooling load. Within each unit, two independent CHW coil circuits share the same coil matrix — an
A-circuit and a B-circuit. The automatic changeover valve switches between A and B CHW sources within 5 seconds of either plant
fault signal, using residual coil thermal mass to bridge the transition without air-side temperature deviation.
Cooling coil: 10-row, 14 fins per inch, aluminium-fin copper-tube; two independent CHW circuits sharing the coil matrix; face area
2,200 mm x 1,500 mm; CHW supply 6°C / return 12°C for both circuits. Each circuit carries 100% of unit thermal duty independently.
Fan section: four (4) EC backward-curved fans per unit, dual-power-feed motor connections for 2N electrical power path compliance.
Each fan motor has two independent power connection terminals; loss of either power path does not affect fan operation.
CHW changeover valve: spring-return type, fail-to-CHW-Plant-A position. In the event of both control power and actuator signal
failure, the valve defaults to Plant A — the designated primary CHW source. Plant B is selected only by an active control signal. This
fail-safe default prevents an ambiguous valve state where loss of control signal could leave both CHW paths interrupted.
Controls: dual-processor group controller with two independent processor boards, dual power supply, and dual Ethernet network
ports on separate VLANs. Loss of any single controller component does not interrupt control function; secondary processor assumes
active role within 2 seconds of primary fault. BACnet IP transmitted on both network interfaces simultaneously.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 100 mm double-wall insulated; seismic SDC D provisions for Chicago per IBC 2021. Certified
seismic calculations signed by a licensed structural engineer included in engineering package.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-13, redundant dual-track allowing online filter replacement without airflow interruption. Each unit has two
parallel filter tracks; one track is bypassed during replacement while the other maintains full filtration.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Tier 4 certification requirement: the Uptime Institute's Tier 4 standard requires the facility to maintain cooling operation through any
single component failure. TMI's dual-circuit coil, dual-processor controller, and dual-feed fan motors address individual component
failures within the AHU itself, complementing the Plant A/B redundancy at the facility level.
(cid:127) 5-second changeover valve timing: the coil thermal mass at design cooling conditions retains enough cold stored energy to
maintain supply air temperature within 2°C of setpoint for approximately 8 to 12 seconds after CHW flow is interrupted. The 5-second
changeover valve timing provides a 3 to 7-second margin — verified at factory test by interrupting Plant A CHW flow on a fully loaded
unit.
(cid:127) Dual-processor controller failover: the two processor boards run synchronised state machines, with the secondary board monitoring
the primary board's heartbeat at 100 ms intervals. If three consecutive heartbeats are missed, the secondary board assumes active
control and transmits a BACnet alarm to the DCIM platform without interrupting unit set-points. Failover event is logged with a
timestamp for post-event analysis.
(cid:127) Chicago seismic SDC D: Chicago's ASCE 7-22 Seismic Design Category is D, driven by the New Madrid Seismic Zone. SDC D
requires seismic restraint analysis to 1.0g horizontal and 0.67g vertical equivalent static force. TMI's SDC D restraint bracket is a
bolted steel weldment anchored to the concrete slab; the certification calculation is signed by a licensed structural engineer and
included in the building permit package.
(cid:127) Insurance underwriter requirements: the underwriter's risk engineer specified that the factory test must include a simultaneous dual
fault — not sequential faults with recovery between them — at a minimum of 75% cooling load. TMI's test protocol met both
conditions; the test report was co-signed by TMI's quality manager and the underwriter's representative.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was designing the CHW changeover valve as a spring-return
fail-to-Plant-A device rather than a motorised proportional valve, ensuring that loss of both control power and control signal in the
same fault event defaults to a defined CHW source rather than leaving valve position indeterminate — satisfying the Tier 4
requirement that no single control fault can cause a simultaneous loss of both CHW supply paths.
OUTCOME: WON
Awarded after the customer's insurance underwriters reviewed TMI's factory Tier 4 dual-fault simulation protocol. The underwriters
required evidence that zero cooling interruption would occur through any combination of two simultaneous failures — a single active
unit failure plus a simultaneous chilled water plant failure. TMI's factory test demonstrated this scenario on a 4-unit representative
group; competing bids did not include a factory witness test protocol of equivalent scope. The underwriter's approval of the preferred
insurance rate was contingent on this test evidence, making it a binding condition of the award.
