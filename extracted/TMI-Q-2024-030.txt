1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 7,500 kW sensible — four-building campus, all phases, under a single master purchase agreement. Twenty-four
(24) large chilled water AHUs at 375 kW each, deployed as Tier 4 2N across four buildings: six active plus six standby per building,
with each standby unit paired to mirror one specific active unit.
Master programme architecture: all 24 units share identical coil specification, fan and motor assembly, VSD model, CHW valve size,
BACnet point map, and filter frame. This campus-wide parts commonality means a single critical spare parts kit covers the entire
four-building campus — 2 fan/motor assemblies, 4 VSDs, 6 CHW valves with actuators, 1 controller PCB. No building-specific spares
inventory is required.
Cooling coil: 8-row, 12 fins per inch, aluminium-fin copper-tube; dual A and B CHW circuits per unit; face area 2,000 mm x 1,350 mm;
CHW supply 7°C / return 14°C. Waterside economiser per unit: free-cooling active when outdoor WB is below 10°C — approximately
2,800 hours per year in Northern Virginia.
Fan section: three (3) EC backward-curved fans per unit, 42,000 CFM total, dual-power-feed motor connections for 2N electrical path
compliance.
2N changeover valve: spring-return fail-to-Plant-A, 5-second switchover. Identical valve specification across all 24 units for parts
commonality.
Controls: building-level 2N dual-processor group controllers (four buildings, one controller per building); BACnet IP; DCIM API with
campus-level aggregation to a campus energy dashboard. The dashboard provides building-level and campus-level kWh, PUE, and
cooling efficiency metrics. Dashboard licence is included for 3 years.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 100 mm double-wall insulated; seismic SDC B per ASCE 7-22 for Northern Virginia.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-13 with MERV-8 pre-filter, redundant dual-track for online replacement. Identical filter specification across all
24 units.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Campus-wide parts commonality: all 24 units across four buildings share the same coil specification, fan/motor assembly, VSD,
CHW valve, and controller module. A single campus-wide critical spare parts kit covers every unit in the programme; no
building-specific spares inventory is required. This consolidation reduces annual spares carrying cost by approximately CAD 18,000
per year versus a building-by-building independent spares strategy.
(cid:127) Waterside economiser at 2,800 annual hours: at 24 active units, campus-wide annual economiser energy saving is approximately
24 x 180 MWh = 4,320 MWh per year. At Northern Virginia grid electricity rates, this represents approximately USD 345,000 in
annual avoided energy cost — meaningful at the CFO level.
(cid:127) Price lock value: TMI's 18-month price lock eliminates the procurement risk associated with steel, aluminium, and copper price
volatility, which can move coil and cabinet costs by 8 to 15% over an 18-month period. The customer's CFO valued this price
certainty at approximately USD 2.1 million versus the expected cost of escalation clauses in competing building-by-building
quotations.
(cid:127) Northern Virginia reference: TMI has 28 units currently in service across three NoVA installations — Leesburg Data Park (2020, 6
units), Manassas Carrier Hotel (2021, 4 units), and Loudoun Campus Block 1 (2022, 8 units) — with zero warranty claims filed to
date. All three reference facilities have experienced summer peak ambient events reaching 36°C DB and winter events below -5°C
without a unit fault.
(cid:127) Campus energy dashboard: the dashboard aggregates kWh, PUE, and cooling efficiency metrics from all four buildings via BACnet
IP. Building-level PUE is calculated in real time from the integrated power meters on each AHU plus the CHW plant power data feed
(customer to provide plant feed). The dashboard is TMI's proprietary tool; a 3-year licence is included in the quoted price.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was specifying identical equipment across all 24 units in all four
buildings — using the same coil dimensions, fan assembly, VSD model, CHW valve, and filter frame throughout the campus — rather
than optimising each building's specification independently, because the operational value of a single campus-wide critical spare
parts kit, a maintenance team trained on one equipment configuration, and an 18-month price lock on a standardised BOM exceeds
the incremental efficiency gains available from per-building re-optimisation.
OUTCOME: WON
A single-vendor campus-wide programme structure — locking in pricing, lead times, and spare parts commonality across all four
buildings under one master purchase agreement — combined with TMI's Northern Virginia track record of 28 units in service without
a warranty claim, made a total-cost-of-ownership case that the customer's board approved unanimously. The competing proposals
were building-by-building quotations without price lock commitments; the customer's CFO valued the 18-month price certainty at
approximately USD 2.1 million in avoided procurement risk.
