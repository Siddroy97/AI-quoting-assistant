1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 5,800 kW sensible with 1,800 kW latent removal — Phase 1 of a 24,000 kW total campus. Twenty (20) large chilled
water AHUs in 2N configuration; ten active, ten standby. Entering air 26°C DB / 65% RH — Singapore wet-season design. CHW
supply 6°C / return 12°C. Singapore's 27°C WB drives a dominant latent load; the coil selection is governed by latent removal
requirements more than sensible capacity.
Deep dehumidification coil: 10-row, 14 fins per inch, epoxy-coated aluminium fins for tropical corrosion resistance; face area 2,600
mm x 1,800 mm; CHW supply 6°C / return 12°C. Leaving air condition: 12°C DB / 10°C DP. The 10°C DP target requires the coil
surface temperature (approximately 8°C at 6°C CHW supply) to be well below the entering air dew-point of approximately 18°C at
65% RH — producing aggressive condensation across the full 10-row fin depth.
Corrosion protection — full tropical package: epoxy-coated coil fins (25-micron polymer barrier against salt aerosol and coastal
atmospheric corrosion); e-coat cabinet interior (20-micron electro-deposited polyurethane on all steel surfaces); stainless steel
condensate drain pan; tropical Class H motor windings; tropical-grade cabinet door and panel seals.
Dual CHW path for 2N compliance: each unit connected to independent CHW Plants A and B; spring-return fail-to-Plant-A
changeover valve, 5-second switchover. Dual-power-feed fan motors for 2N electrical path compliance.
Fan section: four (4) EC backward-curved fans per unit, 72,000 CFM total at 0.95 in. w.g., tropical Class H motor windings,
dual-power-feed.
Controls: 2N dual-processor group controller, BACnet IP, redundant network, DCIM API; CHW A/B changeover within 5 seconds;
dew-point control with 0.5°C resolution sensors. SS 564 Singapore Standard electrical compliance documentation included.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-13, moisture-resistant polyester media, redundant dual-track. Polyester media specified because cellulose
MERV-13 media loses structural integrity when saturated during Singapore's frequent high-humidity building start-up events.
Shipping: CIF Singapore via TMI's established Singapore freight partner. Anti-humidity sea-freight packaging on all units. Transit time
from Montreal to Singapore port approximately 28 days via Panama Canal.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Singapore ASHRAE 0.4% design: 35°C DB / 27°C WB. The 27°C WB is the design driver for this project. At 27°C WB, outdoor air
moisture content is approximately 18.5 g/kg — one of the highest in any major market TMI serves. The dominant load is latent; the
design SHR for this installation is approximately 0.76, meaning 24% of the total cooling load is latent removal.
(cid:127) Leaving dew-point of 10°C at 6°C CHW supply: the coil surface temperature at 6°C supply is approximately 8°C. Entering air at
26°C DB / 65% RH has a dew-point of approximately 18°C; condensation on the fin surface begins immediately on the first row and
continues aggressively through all 10 rows. The 10-row depth is the minimum required to achieve 10°C leaving DP at this entering
condition.
(cid:127) E-coat and epoxy fin tropical corrosion validation: TMI's Miami reference installations (TMI-Q-2023-014, 4 units, commissioned
2023) are the closest climate analogue to Singapore with comparable salt aerosol concentration. The Miami units were inspected at
12 months post-commissioning and showed zero measurable fin corrosion and no e-coat delamination. This inspection report was
provided to the customer's engineering team during technical evaluation.
(cid:127) SS 564:2010 Singapore Standard compliance: TMI has completed SS 564-compliant electrical documentation for two prior
Singapore installations. All electrical panels are wired to SS 564 requirements for equipment installed in Singapore. The SS 564
inspection body (SAC-accredited) appointment must be confirmed by Week 4 of the engineering phase.
(cid:127) CIF delivery via Singapore logistics partner: TMI uses a Montreal-based freight forwarder with a Singapore receiving agent for CIF
delivery. Transit time from TMI Montreal to Singapore port is approximately 28 days via Panama Canal. Marine insurance is included
in CIF terms at replacement value plus 10%.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was specifying a 10-row deep dehumidification coil at 6°C/12°C
CHW delta-T as the base design — rather than a standard 8-row coil with supplementary dehumidification — because Singapore's
27°C WB entering condition requires aggressive condensation across the full fin depth from the first coil row, and a shallower coil
does not provide sufficient contact time at the 8°C fin surface temperature to achieve the 10°C leaving dew-point target.

Awarded in a field of five bidders. TMI's tropical climate 2N engineering expertise — specifically the factory-demonstrated dual-fault

(TMI-Q-2023-014) as evidence that TMI's epoxy fin and e-coat tropical corrosion protection had been validated in a comparable
high-humidity coastal environment.
