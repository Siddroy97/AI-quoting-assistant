1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 900 kW sensible at chilled water design conditions — CHW supply 7°C / return 14°C, entering air 24°C DB / 50%
RH. Three (3) large chilled water AHUs at 450 kW sensible each, N+1 configuration: two active units carry the full load, one unit on
168-hour auto-standby rotation.
Configuration: Three (3) large floor-mounted chilled water air handling units installed in a dedicated mechanical room adjacent to the
data hall. Supply is distributed via an overhead perforated-ceiling diffuser array; return air flows back through an underfloor plenum.
This separation of mechanical room from live data hall allows coil and filter maintenance without entering the IT space.
Cooling coil: 8-row, 12 fins per inch, aluminium-fin copper-tube; face area 1,800 mm x 1,200 mm. At 7°C/14°C CHW delta-T the coil
achieves a leaving air condition of 13°C DB / 11°C DP — adequate for Dallas humidity control without a supplementary
dehumidification stage.
Fan section: two (2) backward-curved EC plenum fans per unit, direct-drive, variable-speed drives integrated into the motor frame.
Sized for 42,000 CFM at 0.80 in. w.g. combined coil, filter, and distribution resistance. At 50% cooling load, fan speed reduces to
approximately 70% via supply-air temperature control, cutting fan energy by 66%.
Humidification: electrode steam humidifier, 8 kg/hr per unit, for winter-season dew-point supplementation when Dallas outdoor DB
drops to its 99.6% winter design of -1°C. Humidifier engages automatically when supply air dew-point drops below 10°C.
Controls: N+1 group controller, BACnet IP, 168-hour rotation, supply air temperature and dew-point control, power metering per unit
via integrated kWh meter with Modbus TCP output.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 100 mm double-wall insulated panels; side access doors on both faces for coil pull access. Unit
weight approximately 2,800 kg; floor loading confirmation required.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-13 filters. Dallas cedar pollen season (December-February) warrants MERV-13 even in a recirculating
installation due to high infiltration rates through the mechanically ventilated mechanical room.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) CHW design conditions: 7°C supply / 14°C return, 7°C delta-T. Coil selections are optimised for this delta-T; if the CHW plant
delivers supply above 8°C, the leaving dew-point rises above 12°C and humidity control at Dallas summer conditions will be
marginal. The customer's mechanical engineer should confirm chiller setpoint and pipe insulation standard before finalising the CHW
plant design.
(cid:127) N+1 logic at 900 kW total load: each active unit runs at 450 kW — 100% of its rated capacity. Unlike smaller N+1 systems where
units run at 50% with spare capacity headroom, this system is capacity-limited on the active units. The standby unit must be healthy
and ready to assume full load at all times; the group controller self-tests standby unit fan operation and CHW valve response before
each 168-hour rotation.
(cid:127) Electrode humidifier water quality: Dallas municipal water hardness ranges from 100 to 180 ppm as CaCO3. Electrode humidifiers
operate reliably at 100 to 300 ppm hardness; below 100 ppm the conductivity is insufficient for reliable electrode current flow, and
above 350 ppm scale build-up on electrodes accelerates replacement frequency. Dallas water quality is within the acceptable range
and no water treatment is required for this application.
(cid:127) Electric pre-heat section is for cold-start and freeze protection only; it is not sized for space heating. It is interlocked with the CHW
valve to prevent simultaneous heating and cooling, and it energises only when entering air temperature drops below 5°C.
(cid:127) Unit weight 2,800 kg each: customer must confirm floor slab loading capacity with structural engineer before finalising unit
placement. TMI provides a six-point load distribution drawing in the submittal package.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was pairing the 8-row, 12 FPI chilled water coil at 7°C/14°C
delta-T with an independent electrode steam humidifier on each unit rather than specifying a single central humidifier, ensuring
dew-point control and winter humidity supplementation are maintained independently for each unit so that the N+1 rotation schedule
does not create dew-point excursions when the standby unit assumes full humidification load during a Dallas winter cold snap.
