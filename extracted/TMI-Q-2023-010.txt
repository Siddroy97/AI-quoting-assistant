1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 460 kW sensible guaranteed at Las Vegas extreme ambient — outdoor DB 47°C / WB 22°C, entering air 24°C DB /
40% RH. This is among the most thermally demanding air-cooled DX specifications in North America. Four (4) units at 115 kW each
at 47°C ambient, N+1: three active, one standby.
Configuration: Four (4) roof-mounted split DX precision cooling units engineered for sustained operation at 47°C outdoor dry-bulb. At
this ambient condition, a standard R-410A scroll compressor operating at nameplate discharge pressure reaches its high-pressure
lockout limit within 30 minutes of operation without custom thermal management. TMI's high-ambient design addresses this through
three coordinated mechanisms.
High-ambient mechanism 1 — sub-cooled liquid line: each refrigerant circuit is equipped with a liquid-line sub-cooling coil that
reduces refrigerant temperature by approximately 10°C before the thermal expansion valve. At 47°C ambient, sub-cooling to 42°C
recovers 14% of capacity lost to elevated condensing temperature and reduces compressor discharge pressure by 220 kPa.
High-ambient mechanism 2 — oversized condenser face area: condenser coil face area is specified at 22% above the calculated
minimum for 47°C heat rejection. This reduces condensing temperature to approximately 48 to 50°C, maintaining a 100 to 150 kPa
margin below the high-pressure lockout threshold.
High-ambient mechanism 3 — hot-gas bypass valve: at part-load conditions above 40°C ambient, the compressor unloading
characteristic can drive evaporating pressure below the low-pressure cutout. The hot-gas bypass valve injects a controlled flow of
superheated discharge gas into the suction line, maintaining suction pressure above 850 kPa during low-load high-ambient
conditions.
Refrigerant circuit: R-410A, high-ambient scroll compressors rated to 54°C ambient, 8-row aluminium-fin copper-tube evaporator coil
at 14 FPI. 14 FPI specified over standard 12 FPI to increase coil surface area within the same cabinet depth, improving heat transfer
at the elevated delta-T of high-ambient operation.
Filtration: dual-stage — MERV-4 sand and coarse dust pre-filter on outdoor condenser inlet; MERV-11 main filter on indoor
evaporator section. Las Vegas desert dust blinds standard MERV-8 to MERV-11 filters at 4 to 6 times the rate of typical urban
environments; pre-filter replacement interval estimated at 5 weeks during June through September dust season.
Controls: N+1 group controller, BACnet IP, with dedicated high-ambient mode logic that activates sub-cooling circuit, increases
condenser fan speed to maximum, and engages hot-gas bypass modulation automatically when outdoor DB sensor exceeds 40°C.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Las Vegas ASHRAE 0.4% cooling design: 47°C DB / 22°C WB. The 47°C DB is the highest 0.4% design condition in the continental
US for major cities. The low WB (22°C) means the air is extremely dry at this temperature — the combination of extreme dry-bulb and
low humidity is unique to the Mojave Desert and requires a design approach fundamentally different from humid-climate high-ambient
strategies.
(cid:127) R-410A operating limits at 47°C ambient: the compressor high-pressure cutout at 4,150 kPa corresponds to a saturated condensing
temperature of approximately 52°C. At 47°C ambient with standard condenser sizing, the condensing temperature reaches 54 to
56°C within 20 minutes, tripping the lockout. The sub-cooling and oversized condenser combination reduces condensing temperature
to 48 to 50°C, restoring a 2 to 4°C margin below the lockout threshold.
(cid:127) Hot-gas bypass valve calibration: factory-calibrated for a suction pressure of 850 kPa minimum. At 47°C ambient, the combination
of high head pressure and low load can drive suction pressure below 850 kPa without bypass. The valve injects sufficient hot gas to
maintain 850 kPa suction pressure across the full load range from 30% to 100%.
(cid:127) 14 FPI coil selection: increasing fin density from 12 to 14 FPI adds approximately 17% to coil heat transfer surface area within the
same cabinet depth. At 47°C ambient the sensible heat ratio is higher than at cooler conditions; the additional surface area maintains
latent removal at an adequate level for data centre humidity control.
(cid:127) Custom design premium: the combination of high-ambient compressors, oversized condenser, sub-cooling circuit, hot-gas bypass,
and 14 FPI coil adds approximately CAD 7,400 per unit versus a standard catalogue selection. This premium represents genuine
engineering value but is difficult to justify in a capital-cost-only procurement where a de-rated standard unit is accepted as a
compliant alternative.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was combining three independent high-ambient mechanisms —
sub-cooled liquid line, oversized condenser face area, and hot-gas bypass — rather than relying on a single oversizing factor,
because each mechanism addresses a distinct thermodynamic failure mode that the other two cannot prevent at 47°C sustained
operation, and any single-mechanism approach would require component sizes that do not fit within the standard cabinet envelope.
