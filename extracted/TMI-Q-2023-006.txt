1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 380 kW sensible at Austin design conditions — outdoor DB 40°C / WB 24°C, entering air 24°C DB / 50% RH. Three
(3) roof-mounted split DX units at 190 kW each, N+1: two active units carry the 380 kW load while the third rotates on a 240-hour
auto-standby cycle.
Configuration: Roof-mounted split DX precision cooling units with indoor evaporator sections floor-mounted for underfloor supply
through raised floor plenum at 0.07 in. w.g. static, overhead return. Remote condenser sections are roof-mounted on structural curbs
with 50 mm thermal-break seals to limit radiant heat gain through the roof interface during Austin's extended high-ambient season.
Refrigerant circuit: R-410A, high-capacity dual-circuit scroll compressor arrangement per unit, 8-row aluminium-fin copper-tube
evaporator coil at 12 FPI. The 8-row coil depth is specified over the more common 6-row because Austin's 24°C WB design condition
imposes a meaningful latent load; the additional coil rows deepen the fin temperature depression and improve moisture removal at
part-load.
Condensing: Remote air-cooled condenser, EC fans with variable-speed head-pressure control, roof-mounted, one per indoor unit.
Condenser coil face area includes a 12% oversizing buffer relative to the calculated minimum at 40°C ambient, preventing
condensing pressure from approaching the high-pressure lockout threshold during multi-day heat events when Austin outdoor DB
can sustain 42°C for 24 to 48 hours.
Airflow: 18,000 CFM per unit at 0.07 in. w.g. underfloor plenum static. Backward-curved plug fan, direct-drive EC motor, variable
speed. N+1 group controller coordinates fan speed across active units to maintain balanced underfloor pressure distribution.
Controls: N+1 group controller, BACnet IP, 240-hour auto-rotation, auto-failover on unit fault within 20 seconds. Return-air dew-point
monitoring; high dew-point alarm at 14°C DP forwarded to BMS.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 75 mm double-wall insulated. Thermal-break roof curb per unit.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-11 with differential pressure monitoring. Austin cedar pollen (December–February) and oak pollen
(March–April) seasons accelerate filter loading; estimated replacement interval 8 weeks during pollen seasons versus 14 weeks
otherwise.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Austin ASHRAE 0.4% cooling design: 40°C DB / 24°C WB. The 24°C WB is the key coil selection driver. At 24°C WB the outdoor
moisture content is approximately 15.5 g/kg, imposing a latent load on the data hall through infiltration around raised-floor cutouts
and door seals. The 8-row coil improves latent removal by lowering the fin surface temperature below the entering air dew point
across a greater fin depth.
(cid:127) Condenser face area buffer of 12%: Austin's high-ambient season (May through September) means the condenser operates near
its design limit for approximately 1,800 hours per year. Without a buffer, partial fin fouling or a single failed EC fan motor would push
head pressure into the high-pressure lockout band.
(cid:127) 240-hour rotation interval was selected over the more common 168-hour cycle because the customer operates lights-out with
remote monitoring only. A 240-hour interval reduces automatic standby-to-active transitions by 30%, lowering compressor start-up
stress cycles while maintaining adequate run-hour equalisation.
(cid:127) Enhanced warranty rationale: scroll compressors in Texas climate zones experience higher thermal cycling stress from Austin's
combination of prolonged high-ambient operation and significant winter shutdown periods. The compressor replacement provision
addresses the statistical mode of failure for this climate class.
(cid:127) Refrigerant recharge provision covers the most common field service call in the 3 to 5 year post-commissioning period, when
refrigerant migration through Schrader valve cores and flare fittings becomes detectable — eliminating a predictable near-term
operating cost from the customer's budget.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was specifying an 8-row evaporator coil depth rather than the
standard 6-row, and pairing it with a 12% oversized condenser face area — a combination that increases material cost by
approximately CAD 2,800 per unit but is the minimum specification to guarantee both adequate latent removal at Austin's 24°C WB
design condition and stable head pressure during the sustained 38°C-plus events that define Austin's summer thermal profile.
OUTCOME: WON
Awarded on the basis of the 24-month enhanced warranty package, specifically the inclusion of one compressor replacement and
one refrigerant recharge event per unit within the warranty period at no additional charge. Two competing bids were technically
comparable with standard 18-month parts-only warranties. The customer's procurement committee scored warranty coverage as a
separate weighted criterion and TMI's extended coverage broke the technical tie.
