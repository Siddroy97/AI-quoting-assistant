1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 300 kW sensible at Boston design conditions — outdoor DB 33°C / WB 23°C, entering air 23°C DB / 50% RH.
Three (3) floor-mounted units at 150 kW each, N+1: two active units carrying 300 kW, one unit on auto-standby with 15-second
failover.
Configuration: Floor-mounted split DX precision cooling units, underfloor supply through raised-floor plenum, overhead hot-return.
Remote air-cooled condenser sections, roof-mounted, one per indoor unit. Seismic restraint provisions per ASCE 7-22 for Boston
Seismic Design Category B — all units supplied with certified four-point floor anchor brackets and a seismic restraint calculation
package for submission to the Massachusetts AHJ.
Refrigerant circuit: R-410A, dual-circuit scroll compressors per unit, 6-row aluminium-fin copper-tube evaporator coil. Dual circuits
ensure graceful 50% degradation on single compressor fault — critical in a financial services environment where cooling interruption
can trigger a trading halt.
Airflow: 13,500 CFM per unit at 0.60 in. w.g. underfloor plenum static. EC fan motor explicitly sized for MERV-13 filter pressure drop
of 0.28 in. w.g. at clean-filter condition — not a re-used MERV-8 standard motor selection.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-13, front-access. MERV-13 is the customer's internal standard for all mechanical equipment in this facility,
adopted in response to Massachusetts building code requirements for commercial occupancies.
Controls: N+1 group controller, BACnet IP, 15-second auto-failover, 168-hour standby rotation. Eight dry-contact alarm points per
unit wired to the building's financial-grade monitoring panel: supply air high temperature, return air high temperature, refrigerant high
pressure, refrigerant low pressure, filter differential high, condensate overflow, unit fault, and group controller communications loss.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 75 mm double-wall insulated. Certified seismic floor anchor kit, four-point. Anchor bolt pattern
matches standard data centre raised-floor pedestal grid (600 mm on centre) to simplify installation.
Condensing: Remote condenser, roof-mounted, EC fans with variable-speed head-pressure control. Boston summer design is
moderate at 33°C DB; condenser selection includes 10% capacity margin as standard.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Boston ASHRAE 0.4% cooling design: 33°C DB / 23°C WB. Moderate summer climate; the governing design parameters for this
project are seismic compliance, MERV-13 filtration pressure drop, and delivery schedule — not the thermal performance envelope.
(cid:127) Seismic Design Category B per ASCE 7-22 for Boston corresponds to a short-period spectral acceleration Ss of approximately
0.35g. The four-point anchor bracket is designed for 0.5g horizontal and 0.33g vertical equivalent static force, providing margin above
SDC B requirements.
(cid:127) MERV-13 fan motor sizing: a MERV-13 filter at design airflow adds 0.28 in. w.g. to the fan's external static pressure versus MERV-8
at the same face velocity. The EC fan motor on this unit was sized at quotation stage for the MERV-13 resistance. Using a
MERV-8-rated motor with MERV-13 filters installed would reduce airflow by approximately 12% below design — a common
installation error on projects where filtration specification changes late in the design process.
(cid:127) Expedited schedule mechanics: TMI pre-purchases key long-lead components before drawing approval. The customer accepts the
engineering risk that a post-approval drawing change may require replacement components. This risk is considered low for a
standard unit configuration but is noted in the commercial terms for the record.
(cid:127) Eight-point alarm monitoring: alarm points cover supply air high temperature, return air high temperature, refrigerant high pressure,
refrigerant low pressure, filter differential high, condensate overflow, unit fault, and group controller communications loss — mapping
to the standard point list required by Massachusetts financial services facility compliance frameworks.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was sizing the EC fan motor explicitly for the MERV-13 filter
pressure drop rather than the lower MERV-8 standard, ensuring that 13,500 CFM design airflow is achieved with MERV-13 filters
installed rather than accepting a 12% airflow deficit that would compromise the cooling capacity guarantee — a specification detail
requiring one additional fan motor frame size and adding approximately CAD 480 per unit in material cost.
OUTCOME: WON
Awarded primarily on delivery schedule. TMI confirmed a 6-week expedited delivery in writing with a detailed week-by-week
procurement and production schedule. Competing bids quoted 10 and 12 weeks. The customer's financial services regulator had set
a hard infrastructure compliance go-live date; the 10-week lead time from the next-lowest bidder would have missed this date by 10
days, making it ineligible regardless of price.
