1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 5,200 kW sensible at 48°C outdoor DB / 22°C WB — Phase 1 of a 24,000 kW total campus. Twelve (12) adiabatic
hybrid CHW primary / DX trim AHUs, 530 kW CHW primary and 100 kW DX trim each at 48°C ambient, Tier 3 2N configuration: six
active units, six standby on 96-hour auto-rotation.
Adiabatic pre-cooling system: cellulose evaporative media panels, 150 mm thickness, on condenser air inlet. Activated when outdoor
DB exceeds 38°C per a three-sensor averaged outdoor DB array. Water consumption at 48°C peak: approximately 175 litres per
hour per unit. Potable water supply required at minimum 3.5 bar; recirculated with blowdown at 1,000 µS/cm conductivity. Annual
media replacement is included in the Year 1 through Year 3 warranty.
CHW primary coil: 10-row, 14 fins per inch, epoxy-coated aluminium fins for Phoenix alkaline dust and adiabatic scale resistance;
face area 2,400 mm x 1,600 mm; CHW supply 6°C / return 13°C. Epoxy fin coating is specified because Phoenix alkaline dust
combined with the slightly elevated pH of adiabatic recirculated water creates an accelerated corrosion environment on standard
aluminium fins.
DX trim circuit: R-410A, 2-row coil, single scroll compressor per unit, sub-cooled liquid line, remote air-cooled condenser. The trim
circuit maintains supply air temperature precision during adiabatic system activation and deactivation transitions — periods where
CHW coil delta-T can fluctuate by 1 to 2°C as the condenser inlet temperature changes.
Fan section: four (4) EC backward-curved fans per unit, 60,000 CFM at 0.90 in. w.g. Four-fan fault tolerance: single fan fault reduces
airflow 25%, compensated by adjacent unit speed increase, no failover triggered.
Controls: zone group controller, BACnet IP, DCIM API, 96-hour 2N rotation, outdoor DB sensor array for adiabatic activation, water
consumption monitoring and blowdown control.
Filtration: dual-stage — MERV-4 sand pre-filter on condenser inlet upstream of adiabatic media; MERV-11 main indoor filter. Desert
pre-filter prevents silica dust from clogging the adiabatic cellulose media channels.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, stainless steel adiabatic media frame, desert-grade seals.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Performance guarantee at 48°C DB is supported by factory test data and field data from the Hall D installation (TMI-Q-2023-017, 6
units, commissioned 2023), which experienced two peak ambient events exceeding 47°C during the 2023 operating season with zero
compressor faults or high-pressure lockout events.
(cid:127) Epoxy fin coating for Phoenix alkaline dust: standard aluminium oxide passivation is disrupted by Phoenix desert dust, which has a
pH of approximately 8.5 due to calcium carbonate content from limestone geology. This alkaline particulate accelerates aluminium
corrosion at fin-to-tube bonds when combined with the slightly elevated pH of adiabatic recirculated water. Epoxy coating provides a
chemical barrier that is pH-neutral across the 6 to 9 pH range.
(cid:127) Water quality: total dissolved solids must not exceed 400 ppm; conductivity probe triggers blowdown when conductivity exceeds
1,000 µS/cm. At Phoenix municipal water quality (TDS typically 300 to 450 ppm), blowdown occurs approximately 3 to 4 times per
day at peak adiabatic demand.
(cid:127) 96-hour rotation cycle: at six active units, each unit completes its standby rotation every 24 days. The shorter 96-hour cycle versus
the standard 120-hour cycle is specified because Phoenix summer heat dome events typically last 3 to 5 days; a 96-hour rotation
ensures every unit has exercised its compressor within the week preceding a heat dome event.
(cid:127) Phase 2 through Phase 6 pricing: TMI commits to holding Phase 1 pricing within 8% for subsequent campus phases if purchase
orders are placed within 24 months of Phase 1 delivery — a price stability provision not available from competitors without similar
installed reference volumes in the Phoenix market.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was specifying epoxy-coated aluminium fins rather than
standard aluminium on the CHW primary coil — a corrosion protection measure driven by Phoenix's alkaline desert dust chemistry
interacting with the adiabatic recirculated water, not by a tropical salt environment — recognising that the corrosion mechanism is
chemical rather than marine and requires the same 25-micron epoxy barrier regardless of proximity to the ocean.
