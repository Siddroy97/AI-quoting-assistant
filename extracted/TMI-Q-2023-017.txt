1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 1,700 kW sensible at Phoenix extreme ambient — outdoor DB 48°C / WB 22°C, entering air 24°C DB / 40% RH.
Six (6) hybrid DX/adiabatic AHUs at 340 kW sensible each at 48°C ambient, N+2 configuration: four units active, two units on
standby with 120-hour auto-rotation. N+2 is specified because the customer's insurance coverage requires the system to maintain
above 50% capacity through the simultaneous loss of any two active units.
Adiabatic pre-cooling: the central feature of this design. Cellulose evaporative media panels, 150 mm thickness, are installed on the
condenser air inlet face. When outdoor DB exceeds 38°C, water is distributed over the media surfaces, evaporating into the
condenser inlet airstream and reducing its temperature by 8 to 12°C before the air contacts the condenser coil. This temperature
reduction converts the 48°C ambient problem into a 37 to 39°C effective condenser inlet condition — within the standard R-410A
compressor operating envelope.
Refrigerant circuit: R-410A, dual-circuit scroll compressors per unit, 8-row aluminium-fin copper-tube evaporator coil at 12 FPI, plus
sub-cooled liquid line circuit. Without adiabatic pre-cooling, this ambient would require hot-gas bypass and oversized condensers and
could not sustain rated capacity beyond 2 to 3 hours. With adiabatic pre-cooling, the DX circuit operates within standard parameters.
Water system: stainless steel adiabatic water distribution manifold per unit. Solenoid valve opens on adiabatic activation signal
(outdoor DB above 38°C from a three-sensor averaged array). Conductivity sensor monitors recirculated water; blowdown valve
opens for 30 seconds when conductivity exceeds 1,000 µS/cm. Water consumption at 48°C peak: approximately 160 litres per hour
per unit. Potable water supply required at minimum 3 bar.
N+2 operation: four active units carry 1,700 kW at full load. Loss of any two active units simultaneously leaves two remaining active
units at 680 kW — above 40% of the design load — sufficient to maintain IT equipment below thermal trip thresholds while the two
standby units are brought online within 30 seconds.
Fan section: four (4) backward-curved EC plenum fans per unit, 35,000 CFM total at 0.85 in. w.g. Four-fan design for fault tolerance:
single fan failure reduces airflow 25%, manageable by group controller increasing adjacent unit fan speed — no failover triggered.
Controls: N+2 group controller, BACnet IP, 120-hour rotation. Outdoor DB sensor array (three sensors, averaged) for adiabatic
activation logic to prevent spurious activation from a single sensor fault.
Filtration: dual-stage — MERV-4 coarse dust pre-filter on condenser inlet upstream of adiabatic media; MERV-11 main indoor filter.
Phoenix dust season requires a pre-filter upstream of the adiabatic media to prevent silica dust from clogging the cellulose media
channels.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Phoenix ASHRAE 0.4% cooling design: 43°C DB / 21°C WB. TMI has designed for the 48°C DB extreme condition observed during
the Phoenix heat dome events of 2020, 2021, and 2022, each of which sustained 47 to 48°C DB for 18 to 36 continuous hours.
Designing only to the 0.4% condition would result in compressor lockout for the most critical periods of the year.
(cid:127) Adiabatic pre-cooling thermodynamics: at 48°C DB and 22°C WB, the adiabatic system achieves approximately 40% of the
maximum wet-bulb depression, reducing condenser inlet temperature to approximately 37 to 39°C. This is the key number — 37°C is
within the operating envelope of a well-specified R-410A circuit without high-ambient features. The adiabatic system converts an
extreme 48°C ambient into a routine 37°C design condition.
(cid:127) Water consumption at peak: 160 litres per hour per unit at 48°C sustained. For a 6-unit installation, peak water consumption is 960
litres per hour — approximately 23,000 litres per day during a 2 to 3-week peak ambient period. Annual adiabatic water cost is
estimated at CAD 1,800 per unit at Phoenix municipal water rates — minor relative to the compressor energy savings versus the
alternative of running standard high-ambient DX at 48°C.
(cid:127) Reference site validation: the Tempe Edge Campus (4 units, installed 2021) and Goodyear Logistics Hub (6 units, installed 2022)
are both within Greater Phoenix and experienced 48°C ambient events in 2022 and 2023. Neither site recorded a compressor fault or
high-pressure lockout during these events. TMI has made the commissioning test reports and 12-month fault logs available to the
customer under NDA.
(cid:127) Cellulose media replacement: adiabatic cellulose media in the Phoenix desert environment degrades at approximately 15 to 18%
per year due to silica dust abrasion and scale accumulation. Annual replacement maintains media performance within 5% of new.
The 3-year warranty covers annual replacement for the first three years; after that, media is a maintenance consumable at
approximately CAD 1,800 per unit per year.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was selecting an adiabatic pre-cooling strategy over alternative
approaches of larger condensers or alternative refrigerants, because adiabatic pre-cooling converts the 48°C extreme Phoenix
ambient into an effective 37°C condenser inlet condition that is within the standard R-410A operating envelope — eliminating the
need for custom high-ambient compressors or hot-gas bypass — at a water consumption penalty that is economically negligible
relative to the energy saved.
OUTCOME: WON
Awarded on the basis of TMI's validated performance data at extreme ambient conditions. Two competing bids offered air-cooled
systems rated at 48°C ambient but neither could provide field-measured performance data at sustained ambient above 44°C. TMI
presented commissioning test reports and 12-month operational data from two Greater Phoenix installations running adiabatic hybrid
units at peak events reaching 48°C. The customer's engineering team evaluated these reference data independently and determined
that TMI was the only bidder with a design validated above the theoretical performance guarantee level.
