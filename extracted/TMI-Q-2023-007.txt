1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 160 kW sensible at Minneapolis summer design conditions — outdoor DB 34°C / WB 23°C, entering air 22°C DB /
45% RH. Equipment is designed to operate year-round in Minneapolis's climate, spanning from 34°C summer peaks to winter lows of
-26°C DB. Both the summer cooling design and the winter low-ambient operating envelope have been used to drive the equipment
specification.
Configuration: Two (2) roof-mounted packaged DX units, 80 kW sensible each, N+1 redundancy. One active unit carries the full 160
kW load; the second is on standby with its heaters and controls energised. Standby unit rotates into active service every 336 hours to
maintain compressor oil circulation during extended cold-weather standby periods.
Refrigerant circuit: R-410A, dual-circuit low-ambient rated scroll compressors per unit, confirmed operable to -30°C outdoor DB.
Crankcase heaters on all compressors, wired to remain energised whenever unit power is on but the compressor is off — preventing
refrigerant migration into the compressor oil sump during cold soaks below -10°C.
Winter low-ambient package — three elements: (1) condenser fan cycling control — fans stage off below outdoor DB 5°C to maintain
condensing pressure above 850 kPa; (2) head-pressure control valve — modulates hot gas to maintain compressor discharge
pressure within operating band down to -26°C; (3) electric resistance preheat section, 8 kW per unit, energises when entering air
falls below 5°C to pre-condition supply air before the compressor starts.
Cold-start sequence: if outdoor DB is below 5°C, energise preheat section and run fan at 30% speed for 3 minutes; start compressor
only after entering-air temperature confirms above 10°C; then modulate condenser fans via head-pressure control. This sequence
prevents liquid refrigerant slugging in the compressor scroll elements during cold start.
Controls: Stand-alone BACnet MS/TP unit controller with arctic cold-start logic. 336-hour standby rotation cycle.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 75 mm double-wall insulated with arctic-grade expanded polyurethane core. All cabinet seams
use overlapping thermal-break joints. Insulated thermal-break roof curb per unit.
Filtration: 2-inch MERV-8 filters. Minneapolis spring particulate from agricultural dust and tree pollen: estimated 10-week replacement
interval April through June.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Minneapolis ASHRAE 99.6% winter design: -26°C DB. This is among the most demanding winter conditions for rooftop air-cooled
DX equipment in the continental US. The three-element low-ambient kit — crankcase heaters, head-pressure control valve, and
condenser fan cycling — is the minimum configuration to ensure reliable compressor operation across the full -26°C to 34°C outdoor
temperature range.
(cid:127) Crankcase heater operation: below -10°C, refrigerant vapour pressure drops below the oil vapour pressure in the compressor
sump, causing refrigerant to condense into the oil. On start, this refrigerant-oil mixture can cause hydraulic slug damage to the scroll
elements. Crankcase heaters maintain sump temperature above 25°C regardless of ambient.
(cid:127) Head-pressure control valve: at outdoor DB below 5°C, standard condensers over-reject heat and drive condensing pressure below
the compressor's minimum operating limit of 850 kPa. The hot-gas valve injects superheated discharge gas upstream of the
condenser inlet to maintain condensing pressure within the operating band. Factory-calibrated, field-adjustable within +/- 50 kPa.
(cid:127) 336-hour standby rotation interval is longer than the standard 168-hour cycle used in warmer climates. During Minneapolis winter,
the refrigerant circuit accumulates vapour pressure imbalances from alternating freeze-thaw cycles on outdoor exposed pipe
sections; a 336-hour rotation allows adequate warm-up and pressure equalisation before the unit is called into full active service.
(cid:127) Cold-start simulation at -20°C was conducted using TMI's refrigerant test rig with a chilled-air inlet plenum. The test confirmed a
3-minute preheat period brings entering air to 10°C before compressor start at this ambient, meeting the startup sequence
specification.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was combining three independent cold-weather protection
mechanisms — crankcase heaters preventing refrigerant migration into compressor oil, a head-pressure control valve maintaining
minimum condensing pressure at deep-winter ambient, and a staged electric preheat cold-start sequence — rather than relying on
any single mechanism, because each element addresses a distinct failure mode that the other two cannot prevent in a Minneapolis
-26°C operating environment.
