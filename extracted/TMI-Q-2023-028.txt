1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 2,900 kW sensible — Hall B, Tier 4 financial services data centre. Twelve (12) large dual-circuit chilled water AHUs
in 2N configuration; six active, six standby.
Tier 4 dual-CHW-path design: each unit connected to two independent CHW plants (Plant A and Plant B), each at 100% campus
capacity. Two independent coil circuits per unit; automatic changeover valve, spring-return fail-to-Plant-A, switches CHW source
within 5 seconds of plant fault.
Cooling coil: 10-row, 14 fins per inch, aluminium-fin copper-tube; dual A+B CHW circuits; face area 2,200 mm x 1,500 mm; CHW
supply 6°C / return 12°C.
Fan section: four (4) EC backward-curved fans per unit, 52,000 CFM total, dual-power-feed motor connections for 2N electrical
power path compliance.
Controls: 2N dual-processor group controller, BACnet IP, redundant network; CHW A/B automatic changeover within 5 seconds;
30-second unit failover.
Seismic restraint: BS EN 1998 (Eurocode 8) seismic compliance for London; certified calculations included in engineering package.
CE marking: TMI will CE-mark all electrical control panels to Low Voltage Directive 2014/35/EU and EMC Directive 2014/30/EU; CE
Declaration of Conformity issued at factory test completion.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-13, redundant dual-track for online filter replacement. London climate does not require cold-climate
provisions; winter design is -1°C DB (ASHRAE 99.6%).

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) London ASHRAE 0.4% cooling design: 33°C DB / 20°C WB. Moderate summer climate; the design is not thermally stressed. The
governing Tier 4 requirements drive the engineering complexity of this project, not the ambient conditions.
(cid:127) CE marking for Low Voltage Directive 2014/35/EU: all factory-assembled electrical panels within the unit are CE-marked;
Declaration of Conformity is issued at factory test completion. CE marking is required for import into the UK post-Brexit under the
UKCA marking framework, but UK authorities have maintained temporary recognition of CE marking for mechanical equipment until
2025.
(cid:127) BS EN 1998 (Eurocode 8) seismic restraint: London's seismic hazard is low but Eurocode 8 compliance is required by the building
permit. TMI's seismic restraint calculation is prepared by a chartered structural engineer and submitted with the equipment
documentation.
(cid:127) UK local content policy: the UK Financial Sector Resilience Framework specifies minimum local-content thresholds for critical
infrastructure equipment. Canadian-manufactured equipment does not qualify as European-manufactured content for the purpose of
the 60% EU-origin threshold. This policy was not communicated to TMI in the original RFQ; the specification was issued on technical
terms and TMI prepared a fully compliant technical and commercial proposal.
(cid:127) UK import duties on Canadian-origin equipment: customer to confirm the tariff classification and applicable duty rate with their
customs broker prior to order placement. UK trade agreements in force at the time of this quotation do not provide preferential tariff
treatment for Canadian-manufactured mechanical equipment.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was preparing a fully CE-marked, BS EN 1998
seismic-compliant, dual-processor Tier 4 design that met every stated technical specification in the RFQ — including the CE
Declaration of Conformity and UK-standard O&M; documentation — only to be ineligible due to a Canadian-origin local content
requirement that was not disclosed until after technical evaluation, making this a commercial rather than technical disqualification.
OUTCOME: LOST
Lost because the UK government's procurement policy for critical national financial infrastructure required a minimum 60%
European-manufactured content by value. TMI's Canadian-manufactured units could not satisfy this local content requirement
regardless of technical merit or price. The policy was not stated in the original RFQ and emerged post-submission when the
customer's legal team reviewed the project against the UK Financial Sector Resilience Framework. TMI prepared this quotation in full
and the equipment was technically compliant in all respects.
