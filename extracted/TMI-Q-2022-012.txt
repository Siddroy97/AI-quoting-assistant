1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 1,200 kW sensible — CHW supply 6°C / return 12°C, entering air 23°C DB / 50% RH. Four (4) large chilled water
AHUs at 400 kW sensible each, N+1: three active, one standby on 168-hour auto-rotation. The 6°C/12°C CHW delta-T is tighter than
TMI's standard 7°C/14°C offering; the lower supply temperature achieves a leaving dew-point of 10°C versus 11°C at standard
delta-T, providing margin for Chicago's summer humidity events when outdoor WB can reach 25°C in extreme years.
Configuration: Four (4) large chilled water AHUs in a dedicated mechanical hall behind the data hall. Supply ducted through a 2-hour
fire-rated wall penetration; air-side arrangement mirrors the Building 1 installation to allow the customer's maintenance team to
operate both facilities with the same procedures.
Cooling coil: 10-row, 14 fins per inch, aluminium-fin copper-tube; face area 2,100 mm x 1,400 mm. The 10-row depth and 14 FPI
configuration is required for the tighter 6°C/12°C CHW delta-T; a standard 8-row coil would not achieve the leaving air conditions
required at this supply temperature without exceeding the coil design face velocity.
Fan section: three (3) backward-curved EC plenum fans per unit, 55,000 CFM total at 0.90 in. w.g. A three-fan array is specified
because a single fan failure on a two-fan unit reduces airflow by 50%, triggering an N+1 failover. With three fans, a single failure
reduces airflow by 33% — manageable by the group controller increasing adjacent active-unit fan speed — avoiding an unplanned
standby activation.
Humidification: electrode steam humidifier, 12 kg/hr per unit, for Chicago winter conditions. Chicago winter design is -22°C DB
(ASHRAE 99.6%); the 12 kg/hr capacity is sized to maintain a minimum 8°C supply air dew-point during the coldest design day with
three active units running.
Controls: N+1 group controller, BACnet IP. BACnet point map is identical to the Building 1 installation, allowing the customer's NOC
to monitor Building 2 without software reconfiguration. Energy sub-metering per unit consistent with Building 1 metering
infrastructure.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 100 mm double-wall insulated. Coil-pull clearance provision on the non-duct side: hinged rear
panel and coil guide rail allow coil replacement without unit relocation.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-13 with MERV-8 pre-filter cassette. The pre-filter extends MERV-13 service life by capturing coarser
particles; Chicago's urban particulate environment and proximity to the freight rail corridor justify dual-stage filtration.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) 6°C/12°C CHW delta-T is tighter than TMI's standard 7°C/14°C offering. The 6°C supply temperature achieves a leaving air
dew-point of 10°C — 1°C below the standard 11°C — providing a margin for Chicago's summer humidity peaks. The 10-row, 14 FPI
coil is the minimum configuration to achieve this leaving condition at 6°C supply without exceeding 3.5 m/s face velocity.
(cid:127) Three-fan per unit design eliminates the N+1 failover trigger that occurs when a two-fan unit suffers a single fan motor failure. TMI's
Building 1 experience showed that a two-fan unit following a fan motor failure during a July heat event triggered an N+1 failover that
itself caused a brief supply air temperature spike. The three-fan design eliminates this failure mode at the cost of higher initial capital.
(cid:127) Electrode humidifier at 12 kg/hr: sized for the worst-case Chicago winter scenario with three active units running at full capacity and
outdoor DB at -22°C. The 12 kg/hr humidifier is specified with a 20% overcapacity factor to ensure reliable operation at maximum
demand without overloading the electrode assembly.
(cid:127) Coil-pull provision adds approximately CAD 800 per unit in fabrication cost but eliminates the need for a mechanical lift and unit
relocation at the time of first coil replacement, estimated within 15 years of commissioning. The mechanical hall is designed with a
1.5 m clearance corridor behind each unit for this purpose.
(cid:127) Chicago winter freeze risk: CHW plant freeze protection (glycol content or trace heat on coil connections) is outside TMI scope. TMI
specifies a CHW coil freeze protection set-point at 3°C coil entering air temperature; below this threshold the CHW valve opens fully
regardless of cooling demand. The customer's controls contractor must implement this logic in the building management system.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was specifying a three-fan EC array per unit rather than the
standard two-fan configuration, accepting the additional capital cost in exchange for eliminating the N+1 failover trigger that occurs
when a single fan failure on a two-fan unit drops airflow by 50% — a failure mode that caused a supply air temperature excursion in
the Building 1 installation and was considered unacceptable in the Building 2 operational profile.
OUTCOME: WON
Awarded competitively. The customer's engineering team had operated TMI equipment at Building 1 (2019 vintage) and cited
familiarity with TMI's controls interface, BACnet point map, and spare parts programme as a factor in reducing total cost of
ownership. The customer's on-site maintenance team was already trained on TMI equipment; retraining for a different manufacturer's
controls platform carried an implicit cost that their evaluation model reflected.
