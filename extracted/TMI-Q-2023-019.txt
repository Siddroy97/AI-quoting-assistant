1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 1,100 kW sensible — CHW supply 7°C / return 14°C, entering air 23°C DB / 50% RH. Four (4) large chilled water
AHUs at 370 kW sensible each, N+1: three active units, one standby on 168-hour auto-rotation. Original RFQ specified 275 kW per
unit with four active units; the face area increase required for the 10 FPI coil selection increased unit capacity from 275 kW to 370
kW, allowing the load to be carried by three active units with improved margin.
Value-engineered coil specification: 8-row, 10 fins per inch, aluminium-fin copper-tube; face area 1,900 mm x 1,250 mm. The original
specification was 12 FPI at 1,700 mm x 1,150 mm face area. The 10 FPI change reduces fin surface density; to maintain equivalent
total heat transfer area, face dimensions are increased by 12% in each direction. The result is a coil with the same thermal duty but
15% lower airside pressure drop — reducing fan motor input power from 18.5 kW to 16.8 kW per fan at design airflow.
Fan energy benefit of 10 FPI: at design airflow, the 10 FPI coil reduces total system pressure drop by 0.12 in. w.g. per unit. This
translates to a fan speed reduction of approximately 6%. Fan power scales as the cube of speed: a 6% speed reduction cuts fan
power by approximately 17%, or 3.1 kW per fan unit. With two fans per unit and three active units, annual fan energy saving is
approximately 147,000 kWh per year — meaningful at Ohio's electricity rate.
Fan section: two (2) backward-curved EC plenum fans per unit, 38,000 CFM at 0.68 in. w.g. system resistance versus 0.80 in. w.g. in
the original specification. EC motor 18.5 kW, integrated VSD — downsized from the 22 kW specified in the original RFQ. The lower
fan pressure requirement allows the motor to be downsized with no reduction in airflow.
CHW valve: 2-way modulating, 4-inch, spring-return fail-closed. Valve authority maintained at 10% as per TMI design standard. The
larger coil face area does not change the CHW flow rate required — thermal duty is identical — so valve sizing is unchanged from
the original specification.
Controls: N+1 group controller, BACnet IP, 168-hour rotation; supply air temperature and dew-point; energy metering per unit.
Energy metering documents the fan energy saving attributable to the 10 FPI value engineering for the customer's sustainability
reporting.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 100 mm double-wall insulated. The larger coil face area (1,900 mm x 1,250 mm) adds 120 mm
to the unit width versus the original specification — confirmed within the mechanical room layout clearances at the time of the value
engineering proposal.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-11. With the 10 FPI coil, coil face velocity is maintained at 2.7 m/s — within the coil operational range.
MERV-11 is appropriate for this recirculating application.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Columbus ASHRAE 0.4% cooling design: 34°C DB / 23°C WB. Standard mid-continent climate; the design is not driven by ambient
extremes. The 1,100 kW cooling load is the primary dimensioning input and the value engineering opportunity arises from the coil
specification rather than the climate.
(cid:127) 10 FPI versus 12 FPI coil selection: reducing fin density from 12 to 10 FPI reduces the number of fins per unit coil depth by
approximately 17%. Each fin creates a flow channel; at 10 FPI the channels are 17% wider, reducing the air velocity gradient and
friction losses through the fin pack. The measured 15% pressure drop reduction at design face velocity is consistent with TMI's
historical coil test data for 10 FPI versus 12 FPI configurations at equivalent face area.
(cid:127) Fan motor downsizing from 22 kW to 18.5 kW: the lower system pressure resistance allows the fan to deliver 38,000 CFM at 0.68
in. w.g. at a shaft power of approximately 17.2 kW — within the 18.5 kW motor frame without thermal overload. The original 22 kW
motor would have been oversized by 28%, operating at low efficiency in the low-shaft-power range during part-load conditions.
(cid:127) Annual energy saving calculation: fan power reduction of 3.1 kW per fan x 2 fans x 3 active units x 8,760 hours x 0.90 capacity
factor = approximately 147,000 kWh per year. At Ohio's 2023 commercial electricity rate of approximately USD 0.085 per kWh, the
annual energy cost saving is approximately USD 12,500 — a 3.2-year simple payback on a zero-incremental-cost VE change.
(cid:127) VE documentation for sustainability reporting: the energy sub-meter on each unit reports fan energy separately from cooling plant
energy. The customer uses this data in their annual sustainability report to demonstrate the measured performance of the VE coil
selection versus the baseline specification.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was proposing a 10 FPI coil at a 12% larger face area in place
of the specified 12 FPI coil — an unsolicited value engineering change that reduces airside pressure drop by 15% and annual fan
energy by approximately 147,000 kWh with no thermal performance reduction — demonstrating that the lowest-cost selection is not
always the default specification and that coil fin density is an under-examined parameter in standard data centre AHU procurement.
OUTCOME: WON
Awarded after TMI proposed an unsolicited coil specification change that reduced the project cost by 11% without impacting thermal
performance. The customer's original RFQ specified a 12 fins-per-inch coil at the industry standard face velocity. TMI's application
engineering team identified that a 10 fins-per-inch coil at a marginally larger face area delivered equivalent thermal performance at
lower airside pressure drop, reducing annual fan energy consumption by approximately 8%. No other bidder submitted a
value-engineering proposal; the customer's procurement committee cited TMI's proactive technical engagement as the differentiating
factor.
