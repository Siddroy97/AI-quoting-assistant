1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 4,500 kW sensible — Phase 1 (2,250 kW) and Phase 2 (2,250 kW), Atlanta Tier 3 campus delivered under a single
master supply agreement. Twelve (12) large chilled water AHUs per phase (24 total), 300 kW each, Tier 3 2N: eleven active, one
standby per phase; CHW supply 7°C / return 14°C.
Configuration: Large floor-mounted chilled water AHUs, hot-aisle containment, underfloor supply. Phase 1 and Phase 2 equipment is
mechanically identical; phase labelling is for site sequencing only. Zone group controllers are shared infrastructure across both
phases.
Cooling coil: 8-row, 12 fins per inch, aluminium-fin copper-tube; face area 1,800 mm x 1,200 mm; CHW supply 7°C / return 14°C.
Waterside economiser per unit: free-cooling active when outdoor WB is below 13°C — approximately 2,400 hours per year in Atlanta.
Fan section: two (2) EC backward-curved fans per unit, 34,000 CFM total at 0.75 in. w.g., variable-speed drives.
Service package: 5-year preventive maintenance agreement covering all 24 units — four PM visits per unit per year, 24/7 emergency
response with on-site response within 24 hours from TMI's Atlanta spare parts depot, semi-annual coil cleaning, annual refrigerant
check, VSD calibration, and filter replacement at each visit.
Spare parts depot: TMI establishes a dedicated Atlanta depot kit for this programme covering: 2 complete fan/motor assemblies, 4
VSD units, 6 CHW valves with actuators, 1 group controller spare PCB, and 1 year of filter stock. Depot is maintained by TMI
throughout the 5-year PM agreement.
Controls: zone group controller, BACnet IP, DCIM API, 2N rotation logic; expandable for Phase 3 if required. Power metering per unit
for tenant billing.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-13.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Atlanta ASHRAE 0.4% cooling design: 35°C DB / 24°C WB. Waterside economiser contributes approximately 2,400 hours per year
of full or partial free cooling. Estimated annual compressor energy saving per unit is approximately 80 MWh — at campus scale this
represents a meaningful annual operating cost reduction.
(cid:127) 5-year PM agreement total value: CAD 180,000 covering 24 units at 4 visits per unit per year over 5 years — equivalent to
approximately CAD 375 per unit per PM visit. This rate reflects TMI's volume discount for a dedicated Atlanta depot programme
versus call-out PM at standard rates.
(cid:127) Atlanta spare parts depot kit: the on-site depot eliminates the 3 to 5 business day lead time for critical spare parts that would
otherwise be shipped from Montreal. The fan/motor assemblies, VSDs, and CHW valves in the depot cover the statistical top three
failure modes for this equipment class at Atlanta ambient conditions.
(cid:127) Two-phase delivery alignment: Phase 1 and Phase 2 equipment is mechanically identical; the 6-week production gap between
phases allows the customer's installation contractor to complete Phase 1 installation and commissioning before Phase 2 equipment
arrives — avoiding site storage of multiple phases simultaneously.
(cid:127) Phase 3 pricing: TMI commits to holding Phase 1 and Phase 2 pricing within 5% for a Phase 3 addition if purchase orders are
placed within 18 months of Phase 2 delivery.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was bundling the 5-year PM service agreement and Atlanta
spare parts depot into the master supply contract rather than treating them as optional add-ons, because the customer's
total-cost-of-ownership evaluation weighted operational cost predictability over a 5-year period as heavily as capital cost — a
purchasing perspective that equipment-only competitors could not address regardless of their unit pricing.
OUTCOME: WON
A comprehensive 5-year service and preventive maintenance agreement bundled with the equipment supply gave the customer a
total-cost-of-ownership advantage over a lower-priced equipment-only bid. The customer's board prioritised OpEx predictability for a
programme of this scale; the competing bid was 6% lower in capital cost but offered no service provision. The bundled PM
agreement, Atlanta spare parts depot, and 24/7 emergency response commitment were the decisive factors.
