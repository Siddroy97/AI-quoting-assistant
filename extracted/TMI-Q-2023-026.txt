1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 2,400 kW sensible — Hall C scope as originally specified; total campus was rescoped to a 3-hall, 9,000 kW
programme during the quotation period. Eight (8) large chilled water AHUs, 400 kW sensible each, Tier 3 2N configuration: four
active, four standby on 120-hour rotation.
Configuration: Large floor-mounted chilled water AHUs with hot-aisle containment, underfloor supply. Seattle's climate makes this an
exceptional free-cooling application: outdoor wet-bulb is below 10°C for approximately 5,600 hours per year — nearly two-thirds of
annual hours — enabling sustained waterside free-cooling through the CHW plant's fluid cooler bypass circuit.
Cooling coil: 8-row, 12 fins per inch, aluminium-fin copper-tube; face area 2,100 mm x 1,400 mm; CHW supply 7°C / return 14°C. Coil
face velocity is set at 2.8 m/s — below the 3.0 m/s standard — reducing airside pressure drop and fan energy, justified by Seattle's
high proportion of part-load operating hours.
Waterside economiser: modulating bypass valve per unit with partial free-cooling capability — the valve modulates to deliver any
fraction of free-cooling simultaneously with mechanical cooling, optimising the transition between full free-cooling and full mechanical
cooling modes rather than switching abruptly between them.
Fan section: three (3) EC backward-curved fans per unit, 44,000 CFM total at 0.75 in. w.g., dual-power-feed motor connections for
2N electrical compliance.
Controls: zone group controller, BACnet IP, DCIM API, 120-hour 2N rotation, waterside economiser logic with partial free-cooling
modulation.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 100 mm double-wall insulated.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-13, specified for West Coast wildfire smoke season (August-September).

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Seattle ASHRAE 0.4% cooling design: 35°C DB / 23°C WB. The moderate summer peak means this system is thermally
understressed; the 2,400 kW cooling load would be delivered at well below the coil's peak thermal duty for the vast majority of
operating hours. The economiser is the dominant operational feature.
(cid:127) Partial free-cooling modulation: the waterside economiser valve modulates continuously between 0% and 100% free-cooling as the
outdoor WB varies between 10°C and 13°C — the transition band. This eliminates the abrupt compressor start/stop events that occur
with a two-position economiser, reducing compressor cycling stress during the spring and autumn transition seasons when Seattle
WB fluctuates across this band multiple times per day.
(cid:127) MERV-13 for wildfire smoke: the ASHRAE recommendation of MERV-13 minimum for data centres in the western US fire corridor is
particularly relevant for Seattle, which received significant smoke incursion during the August 2020, 2021, and 2022 wildfire seasons.
PM2.5 smoke particles at concentrations observed during these events cause corrosion at solder joints and connector pins within 6
to 12 months of exposure without MERV-13 filtration.
(cid:127) Free-cooling at 5,600 annual hours: estimated annual compressor energy saving per unit is approximately 200 MWh. For an 8-unit
installation the campus-level annual saving is approximately 1,600 MWh — meaningful at Pacific Northwest grid electricity rates and
relevant to the customer's stated net-zero carbon commitment.
(cid:127) Rescoped programme note: this quotation covers Hall C as originally specified; lead time, price, and production slot commitment
apply only to the 8-unit Hall C scope. TMI was prepared to quote the 3-hall 9,000 kW campus but required a new production slot
commitment, which was not available at the time the rescoped RFQ was issued.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was specifying a continuously modulating waterside
economiser valve with partial free-cooling capability rather than a two-position open/close economiser, eliminating the compressor
cycling stress that occurs with abrupt economiser switching during Seattle's frequent spring and autumn periods when outdoor WB
oscillates across the 10°C activation threshold multiple times daily.
OUTCOME: LOST
Lost because the customer rescoped the project from Hall C only to a full three-hall campus during the quotation period and reissued
an RFQ for the combined 9,000 kW scope. By the time the revised RFQ was issued, TMI had committed its production capacity to
another programme and could not guarantee the required lead time for the expanded scope. The customer awarded the combined
campus programme to a manufacturer with sufficient uncommitted production capacity to accept the full three-hall order.
