1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 240 kW sensible at Atlanta design conditions — outdoor DB 35°C / WB 23°C, entering air 22°C DB / 50% RH.
Capacity is distributed across three (3) units at 120 kW each, configured for N+1 active rotation: two units carry the 240 kW load
simultaneously, with the third unit on auto-standby cycling into active service every 168 hours to equalise run-hours.
Configuration: Three (3) floor-mounted split DX precision cooling units deployed in a hot-aisle cold-aisle containment arrangement.
Cold-aisle supply through raised floor plenum at 0.05 in. w.g. static; hot-aisle ceiling return through a fully enclosed containment
chimney. Remote air-cooled condenser sections are roof-mounted on a dedicated equipment pad, one condenser per indoor unit.
Refrigerant circuit: R-410A, dual-circuit scroll compressor arrangement per unit, 6-row aluminium-fin copper-tube evaporator coil at
12 FPI. Each unit maintains two independent refrigerant circuits; loss of one circuit reduces unit capacity to 60 kW and allows the
standby unit to be called into service without dropping below 100% of design load.
Airflow: 11,500 CFM per unit at 0.60 in. w.g. through the raised floor plenum. Backward-curved plug fan, direct-drive EC motor,
variable speed via supply-air temperature control at 16°C setpoint.
Humidity and environmental control: infrared humidifier module, 3 kg/hr per unit, for cold-season dry-air compensation. Electric
reheat section, 12 kW per unit, interlocked with the cooling circuit to prevent simultaneous heating and cooling except during
dehumidification mode.
Controls: N+1 group controller, BACnet IP. Rotation every 168 hours; auto-failover on unit fault within 15 seconds. Differential
pressure sensors monitor filter loading; alarm triggers at 0.35 in. w.g. differential.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 75 mm double-wall insulated, access doors on three sides. Drain pan: stainless steel with float
switch and overflow alarm.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-11 with differential pressure monitoring. MERV-11 selected because this is a recirculating system with no
outdoor air path; MERV-13 would add 0.12 in. w.g. pressure drop, increasing fan energy by approximately 9% annually.
Redundancy tier: N+1. Group controller rotates active units on a 168-hour cycle. On fault of any active unit, the standby unit starts
within 15 seconds automatically.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Atlanta ASHRAE 0.4% design: 35°C DB / 23°C WB. The 23°C WB creates a meaningful latent load from infiltration but does not
require a dedicated dehumidification coil. Electric reheat during dehumidification mode is the appropriate response at this climate
severity.
(cid:127) N+1 rotation at 168-hour intervals ensures all three units accumulate equal compressor run-hours over a 504-hour cycle. Without
rotation, a permanently active unit would reach its first compressor maintenance interval approximately two years ahead of the
standby unit.
(cid:127) Dual-circuit evaporator coil allows 50% capacity graceful degradation on loss of one compressor circuit, keeping the unit in partial
service rather than requiring an immediate standby unit start and reducing the frequency of auto-failover events.
(cid:127) Infrared humidifier is specified over electrode steam because Atlanta municipal water conductivity (typically 280 µS/cm) is within the
infrared lamp operating range; electrode humidifiers require conductivity above 250 µS/cm for reliable arc formation.
(cid:127) Differential pressure filter monitoring is included because Atlanta pollen season (March–May) accelerates filter loading. The 0.35 in.
w.g. alarm threshold provides approximately two weeks of warning before filter loading degrades airflow by more than 5%.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was specifying dual independent refrigerant circuits within each
120 kW unit rather than a simpler single-circuit design, allowing any compressor fault to reduce unit capacity to 50% rather than zero
and eliminating the risk of an unplanned full-load failover event during peak Atlanta summer conditions.
