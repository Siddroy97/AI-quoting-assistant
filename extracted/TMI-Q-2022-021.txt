1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 3,200 kW sensible — Block 1 of a three-block hyperscale campus. Eight (8) large hybrid chilled water primary / DX
trim-cooling AHUs, 500 kW CHW primary and 100 kW DX trim per unit at design conditions, configured in a 2N fully redundant
arrangement: four active units carry the 3,200 kW load simultaneously; four standby units are ready to assume any active unit's load
within 30 seconds.
2N paired-failover architecture: each standby unit is assigned to mirror one specific active unit rather than pooled as shared standby.
This deterministic pairing ensures the group controller's failover logic is unambiguous — any active unit failure triggers one identified
standby unit to start, preventing simultaneous multi-unit start events that can cause electrical demand spikes on the facility's utility
connection.
CHW primary coil: 10-row, 14 fins per inch, aluminium-fin copper-tube; face area 2,400 mm x 1,600 mm; CHW supply 6°C / return
13°C. The 6°C/13°C delta-T achieves a 10°C leaving dew-point for Northern Virginia's summer humidity profile. Waterside
economiser valve on CHW supply: free-cooling mode available when outdoor WB is below 10°C — approximately 2,800 hours per
year in Northern Virginia.
DX trim circuit: R-410A, 2-row trim-cooling coil, single scroll compressor per unit, remote air-cooled condenser. Provides up to 100
kW supplementary cooling to maintain supply air temperature precision during the waterside economiser transition period, when
CHW supply temperature can fluctuate by 1 to 2°C as the fluid cooler bypass circuit equalises.
Fan section: four (4) backward-curved EC plenum fans per unit, 60,000 CFM total at 0.90 in. w.g. Four-fan fault tolerance: loss of one
fan reduces airflow 25%, compensated by group controller increasing adjacent active-unit fan speeds — no failover triggered for a
single fan fault.
Controls: zone-level group controller, BACnet IP with DCIM API, managing four 2N paired units per zone. 30-second failover
guaranteed and verified at factory test. Waterside economiser switchover logic and outdoor WB monitoring included. Power metering
per unit via integrated kWh meter.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 100 mm double-wall insulated; seismic SDC B provisions per ASCE 7-22 for Northern Virginia.
Filtration: 4-inch MERV-13 with MERV-8 pre-filter. Dual-stage filtration for Northern Virginia's spring pollen season — the MERV-8
pre-filter captures coarser pollen particles before they load the MERV-13 media, extending MERV-13 service life from approximately
8 weeks to 14 weeks during the March-to-May season.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Northern Virginia ASHRAE 0.4% cooling design: 36°C DB / 24°C WB. The 24°C WB is the design driver for coil selection; at this
humidity a 10°C leaving dew-point requires a 10-row coil at 6°C CHW supply. The customer's CHW plant has been designed for the
6°C/13°C delta-T used here; this specification is not interchangeable with the standard 7°C/14°C product.
(cid:127) 2N paired failover versus pool standby: pool standby is simpler to programme but introduces a race condition where simultaneous
active unit faults can request more standby units than are available. Paired failover is deterministic — unit A1 always calls
A1-standby, unit B1 always calls B1-standby — ensuring predictable electrical demand during failover events, which is important
where utility demand charges apply.
(cid:127) Waterside economiser at 2,800 annual hours: during these hours the CHW compressors are bypassed and a fluid cooler provides
6°C CHW supply using only fan energy. Annual compressor energy saving per unit is approximately 180 MWh — significant at
campus scale.
(cid:127) DX trim circuit purpose: the 2-row R-410A trim coil handles the 1 to 3°C supply air temperature deviation that occurs during the
waterside economiser switchover transition, typically lasting 3 to 8 minutes as the CHW supply temperature equalises. Without the
trim circuit, supply air temperature can rise to 19 to 21°C during this transition, triggering IT equipment thermal alarms.
(cid:127) Northern Virginia reference installations: Leesburg Data Park (2020, 6 units) and Manassas Carrier Hotel (2021, 4 units). Both sites
have experienced summer peak ambient events reaching 36°C DB and maintained cooling capacity without unit fault. Reference
contacts available to the customer on request.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was implementing deterministic 2N paired-failover logic —
assigning each standby unit to mirror one specific active unit — rather than a pool standby architecture, ensuring predictable
single-unit electrical demand during any failover event and eliminating the race condition where simultaneous multiple faults could
exhaust the standby pool before all affected zones are recovered.
