1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 180 kW sensible at site design conditions — Phoenix, AZ at 1,117 ft (340 m) elevation, outdoor DB 43°C / WB
21°C, entering air 24°C DB / 50% RH.
Configuration: Two (2) roof-mounted packaged direct-expansion units, 90 kW sensible each, arranged in N redundancy. Each
module is fully self-contained with independent refrigerant circuit, condenser, and controls. On loss of one module, the remaining unit
carries the full 90 kW load while the facility operates at reduced capacity pending repair.
Refrigerant circuit: R-410A, dual-circuit scroll compressors per unit, 8-row aluminium-fin copper-tube evaporator coil at 12 fins per
inch. Sub-cooled liquid line kit included on each circuit: liquid refrigerant is cooled an additional 8°C before reaching the thermal
expansion valve, recovering approximately 12% of capacity that would otherwise be lost at 43°C condensing ambient.
Condensing section: Remote air-cooled condenser, roof-mounted adjacent to evaporator section, EC fan motors with variable-speed
head-pressure control. Condenser coil face area oversized by 15% relative to a standard sea-level selection to maintain condensing
pressure within scroll compressor operating envelope at sustained 43°C outdoor dry-bulb without triggering high-pressure lockout.
Airflow: 8,500 CFM per unit at 0.75 in. w.g. external static. Supply through raised-floor plenum, return overhead.
Controls: Microprocessor unit controller per module, BACnet MS/TP, supply-air temperature setpoint control at 16°C, high/low
pressure safeties with automatic reset lockout after three faults within one hour.
Cabinet: 16-gauge galvanised steel, 50 mm double-wall insulated panels, powder-coat RAL 7035. All exterior fasteners stainless
steel — Phoenix UV and thermal cycling environment.
Filtration: 2-inch MERV-8 panel filters, front-access rack. Filter replacement interval estimated at 8 weeks in Phoenix dust season
(June–September) vs 14 weeks balance of year.
Redundancy tier: N — one active unit per zone, one standby. Manual changeover via unit controller; automatic changeover on fault
available as a field-enabled option.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Site design conditions are based on Phoenix Sky Harbor ASHRAE 2021 0.4% cooling hour: 43°C DB / 21°C WB. The low wet-bulb
relative to the extreme dry-bulb is characteristic of the Sonoran Desert climate and is the reason a sub-cooling strategy is viable here
— low ambient moisture means the condenser can reject heat efficiently even at 43°C as long as condensing pressure is managed.
(cid:127) Without the sub-cooled liquid line kit, scroll compressor capacity de-rates by approximately 18% at 43°C condensing versus 35°C
nameplate conditions. The sub-cooling circuit arrests this de-rating and ensures the 90 kW guarantee is met without requiring larger,
more expensive compressor frames.
(cid:127) Condenser coil face area is specified at 115% of the calculated minimum for 43°C rejection. This 15% buffer prevents the
condensing pressure from reaching the high-pressure lockout threshold (4,150 kPa for R-410A) during multi-day heat events where
outdoor DB can reach 46°C for periods of 6 to 18 hours.
(cid:127) EC condenser fans are set to run at 100% speed when outdoor DB exceeds 40°C. Below 35°C, speed modulates down to 60%
minimum; this reduces fan energy during the 7 cooler months when Phoenix DB averages 28°C or below.
(cid:127) Roof structural loading: unit shipping weight is 710 kg per module. Customer's structural engineer of record must confirm slab
capacity before installation. TMI's load distribution drawing is included in the submittal package.
(cid:127) Power supply confirmed as 480V/3ph/60Hz. Unit is not suitable for 208V operation without an external step-up transformer; this
exclusion is noted to avoid field wiring errors.
(cid:127) N redundancy in this application means one unit is active and one is on standby. The customer has opted not to enable automatic
changeover, which reduces the risk of both units attempting to start simultaneously on a momentary power disturbance.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was oversizing the condenser coil face area by 15% and adding
a sub-cooled liquid line circuit on each refrigerant path, rather than accepting a de-rated performance guarantee at 43°C ambient —
a combination that adds approximately CAD 3,200 per unit in material cost but eliminates the risk of compressor high-pressure
lockout during Phoenix summer peak events.
