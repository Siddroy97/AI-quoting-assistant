1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 600 kW sensible — CHW supply 7°C / return 14°C, entering air 23°C DB / 50% RH. Three (3) chilled water AHUs
at 300 kW sensible each, N+1: two active, one standby on 168-hour auto-rotation.
Configuration: Floor-mounted chilled water AHUs, raised-floor supply, ceiling return. This Hall C expansion is mechanically consistent
with Halls A and B at the same facility; unit dimensions, CHW pipe connection sizes, and BACnet point map are matched to the
existing installation to allow the customer's single-person facilities team to operate all three halls with the same maintenance
procedures and spare parts inventory.
Cooling coil: 8-row, 12 fins per inch, aluminium-fin copper-tube; face area 1,600 mm x 1,100 mm; CHW supply 7°C / return 14°C.
Standard delta-T coil matched precisely to Hall A and B specification.
Cold-climate winter package: glycol pre-heat valve on CHW coil water connections — opens when entering air temperature falls
below 4°C to circulate warm glycol through the coil header stubs, preventing freezing during building pressurisation events in
Toronto's -23°C winter design. A glycol supply pressure sensor is wired to an alarm relay that alerts the BMS if glycol pressure drops
below 50 kPa.
Fan section: two (2) backward-curved EC plenum fans per unit, 30,000 CFM at 0.75 in. w.g., variable-speed drives. Fan selection
matched to Hall A and B for parts commonality — identical fan wheel diameter, VSD model, and motor frame.
Controls: N+1 group controller, BACnet IP. BACnet point map is identical to Halls A and B, allowing the customer's existing BMS to
monitor Hall C without software reconfiguration. Bilingual English/French alarm message text — all alarm descriptors appear in both
languages in the controller HMI and in the BACnet alarm log, consistent with Ontario bilingual requirements for commercial facilities.
Cabinet: 14-gauge galvanised steel, 100 mm double-wall insulated, arctic-grade polyurethane seals on all door and panel joints.
Consistent with Hall A and B specification.
Documentation: complete O&M; manuals, as-built drawings, and commissioning records in bilingual English/French format per
Ontario provincial standard. CSA C22.2 electrical certification included as standard for Canadian market.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Toronto ASHRAE 99.6% winter design: -23°C DB. The glycol pre-heat valve is the critical cold-weather protection feature. Without
it, a loss of CHW plant flow during a winter pressurisation event can allow the coil water to freeze within 15 minutes at -23°C entering
air. The valve opens automatically below 4°C entering air, circulating heating glycol regardless of CHW valve position.
(cid:127) Parts commonality with Halls A and B: the Hall C units use identical coil face dimensions, fan wheel dimensions, VSD model, CHW
valve size, and filter frame dimensions to Halls A and B. A single set of critical spare parts — one complete fan/motor assembly, one
VSD unit, one CHW valve with actuator — covers all three halls. The customer currently maintains this spare parts kit for Halls A and
B and confirmed no incremental spares investment is required for Hall C.
(cid:127) Bilingual alarm messages: all alarm text in the BACnet controller HMI is stored in both English and French in the controller's
non-volatile memory. The display language toggles via a front-panel setting; the BACnet alarm log transmits both language strings
simultaneously. This feature is standard on all TMI units for the Canadian market and adds no cost.
(cid:127) CSA C22.2 certification: all electrical panels within the unit are constructed to CSA C22.2 No. 236 for equipment installed in
Canada. CSA certification is required by the Ontario Building Code for mechanical equipment with factory-assembled electrical
panels. TMI's CSA certification is current and transferable to the customer's building permit.
(cid:127) DAP Toronto delivery: TMI's domestic freight rate to the Greater Toronto Area is based on a contracted carrier arrangement with a
Montreal-to-Toronto specialist. Delivery is door-to-door to the specified loading dock address; unloading is the customer's
responsibility. DAP delivery eliminates the need for the customer to source a separate freight carrier.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was matching the Hall C unit specification precisely to the
existing Hall A and B installation — coil dimensions, fan assembly, VSD model, CHW valve size, BACnet point map, and filter frame
— rather than optimising each parameter independently, because the operational value of a shared spare-parts inventory and a
maintenance team already trained on this exact equipment configuration is greater than the incremental efficiency gains available
from a re-optimised selection.

programme where delivery timing was a critical path constraint for the customer's tenant onboarding schedule.
