1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 750 kW sensible — hybrid configuration; 400 kW direct-expansion primary, 350 kW chilled water supplementary;
entering air 23°C DB / 50% RH. Four (4) units at 190 kW each, N+1: three active, one standby. The mechanical room floor plate
imposes a hard 900 mm depth constraint per unit due to structural column spacing; TMI's minimum achievable depth for this hybrid
configuration is 950 mm after two rounds of design revision.
Configuration: Hybrid DX/CHW precision cooling units for a constrained urban mechanical room. The hybrid architecture is specified
because the building's chilled water plant is sized for 50% of the total cooling load; the DX primary circuit provides the balance
without requiring immediate CHW plant expansion.
DX primary circuit: R-410A, 4-row evaporator coil, scroll compressors with hot-gas bypass, remote air-cooled condenser on roof. DX
is the base-load circuit; when outdoor DB exceeds 32°C or DX compressor head pressure rises above a programmed set-point, the
CHW supplementary circuit is engaged.
CHW supplementary circuit: 3-row coil, 2-way modulating valve, CHW supply 7°C / return 14°C. Hybrid controller manages the load
division between DX and CHW circuits to minimise total energy consumption while maintaining supply air temperature setpoint.
Cabinet depth constraint: TMI's standard hybrid unit is 1,200 mm deep. The constrained site requires 900 mm. After two engineering
iterations, TMI achieved 950 mm depth through fan wheel diameter reduction from 630 mm to 540 mm and coil width reduction
compensated by increased row count. The 950 mm unit meets the thermal specification but exceeds the 900 mm hard constraint by
50 mm.
Controls: Hybrid controller, BACnet IP; DX/CHW load split managed by compressor head pressure and CHW valve position. N+1
group controller with 168-hour rotation.
Filtration: 2-inch MERV-11, front-access. Filter rack depth reduced to 55 mm in the narrow-profile cabinet.
Airflow: 22,000 CFM per unit at 0.45 in. w.g. Design airflow is reduced 9% from the standard for this capacity class due to the smaller
fan wheel diameter.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) New York City ASHRAE 0.4% cooling design: 34°C DB / 24°C WB. Moderate summer condition; the design is not thermally
stressed. The governing constraint on this project is the 900 mm depth limit, which has required custom engineering at every
sub-assembly level.
(cid:127) Fan wheel diameter reduction from 630 mm to 540 mm reduces fan wheel tip area by approximately 27%. At the same rotational
speed this reduces maximum achievable airflow by approximately 18%; fan speed is increased to compensate, recovering airflow to
within 9% of the standard. The 9% airflow deficit at design static is acceptable — design cooling capacity at reduced airflow is 190
kW versus 210 kW for the standard product.
(cid:127) Hybrid load split strategy: DX provides base cooling at all times due to its lower operating cost versus CHW in New York's electricity
tariff structure. CHW supplements when DX head pressure is elevated during summer afternoons. The hybrid controller minimises
simultaneous full-load operation of both circuits to avoid overshooting the supply air temperature setpoint.
(cid:127) Phase 2 DX decommissioning provision: the DX compressors are mounted on a removable chassis within the cabinet, accessible
from the rear. When the CHW plant is expanded, the DX chassis can be removed and the unit converted to a standard CHW AHU.
This provision is included in the engineering design at no additional cost.
(cid:127) Column grid constraint: the 900 mm structural column spacing is a concrete slab dimension that cannot be modified without
significant structural cost. TMI's minimum achievable 950 mm product depth made the column constraint technically binding — 50
mm over the available space — after exhausting all available design optimisations.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was attempting to achieve the 900 mm depth constraint through
fan wheel diameter reduction and coil face area optimisation rather than immediately declaring the constraint unachievable — an
engineering approach that produced a 950 mm solution meeting the thermal specification but falling 50 mm short of the architectural
limit, ultimately leaving the project without an award despite two full engineering iterations.
OUTCOME: LOST
Lost despite two rounds of specification revision. The customer's mechanical room floor plate imposes a 900 mm depth constraint
from a structural column grid that cannot be modified. TMI's minimum achievable depth for this hybrid configuration is 950 mm,
adding CAD 4,800 per unit in custom fabrication costs. A competing manufacturer had a proprietary narrow-chassis product line with
an 800 mm standard depth that fit the constraint without a custom engineering premium, and was awarded at a price below TMI's
custom-cabinet quotation.
