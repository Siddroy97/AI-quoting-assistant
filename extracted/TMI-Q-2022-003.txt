1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATION
Cooling capacity: 95 kW sensible guaranteed at site conditions — Denver, CO at 1,609 m (5,280 ft) elevation, outdoor DB 36°C / WB
15°C, entering air 22°C DB / 40% RH.
Configuration: One (1) roof-mounted packaged direct-expansion unit, 95 kW sensible at altitude. The Denver elevation of 1,609 m
reduces ambient air density to approximately 83% of sea-level standard. Every performance figure in this quotation — capacity,
airflow, fan static, condenser rejection — has been corrected for this density reduction. A unit selected on sea-level ratings would
deliver only 79 kW at this site.
Altitude de-rating methodology: compressor volumetric efficiency corrected using the ratio of actual air density (1.020 kg/m3 at
Denver) to standard sea-level air density (1.225 kg/m3). Condenser fan blade pitch increased by 4° from the sea-level setting to
maintain equivalent mass-flow rate at lower air density. Evaporator coil face velocity held constant at sea-level equivalent by reducing
airflow volume by the same density ratio.
Refrigerant circuit: R-410A, dual-circuit scroll compressors, 6-row aluminium-fin copper-tube evaporator coil at 12 FPI. Altitude-rated
compressors confirmed operable down to 1,800 m — providing margin above the Denver site elevation.
Condensing: Remote air-cooled condenser, roof-mounted, EC fans with altitude-corrected blade pitch. Condenser coil selected at
36°C outdoor DB with a minimum 5°C approach to target condensing temperature, ensuring the compressor operates within rated
discharge pressure limits at Denver's combination of moderate dry-bulb and low air density.
Airflow: 4,800 CFM at 0.50 in. w.g. external static — this is the altitude-corrected volume flow; the equivalent mass flow rate is 4,800
x 1.020/1.225 = 3,995 CFM at sea-level equivalent. Fan motor is sized for the volume flow at Denver density, not sea-level mass
flow.
Controls: BACnet IP microprocessor controller; supply-air temperature control at 16°C; altitude compensation algorithm adjusts
refrigerant charge monitoring thresholds — low-pressure cutout set-point corrected for Denver suction pressure.
Cabinet: 16-gauge galvanised steel, 50 mm double-wall insulated, powder-coat RAL 7035. Roof curb with neoprene perimeter seal.
Filtration: 2-inch MERV-8 panel filters. Denver's brown cloud particulate environment: filter replacement interval estimated at 10
weeks.
Redundancy tier: N — single unit, no standby in Phase 1. Customer has requested provision for a second unit in Phase 2; condenser
section structural frame includes a pre-drilled expansion flange to accommodate a second condenser module without modifying the
roof curb.

7. ENGINEERING NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS
(cid:127) Denver ASHRAE 2021 0.4% cooling design: 36°C DB / 15°C WB at 1,609 m elevation. The 15°C WB is unusually low for a location
with a 36°C DB peak, and this combination is characteristic of the Denver semi-arid climate. The low WB means outdoor air is dry
and condenser heat rejection is efficient even at 36°C DB — this is an important distinction from Phoenix, where the high dry-bulb is
the governing constraint. In Denver, air density is the governing constraint.
(cid:127) Air density at 1,609 m and 36°C: 1.020 kg/m3. At sea-level standard (20°C): 1.225 kg/m3. Density ratio: 0.833. This ratio is applied
to compressor volumetric efficiency, condenser airflow mass rate, and evaporator coil psychrometrics. A unit specified on sea-level
ratings delivers 95 kW x 0.833 = 79 kW at this site without altitude correction — a 17% shortfall that would go undetected until
commissioning on a less rigorously specified project.
(cid:127) Condenser fan blade pitch increase of 4°: at 1,609 m, the standard blade pitch moves the same volume of air but at lower mass flow
rate. Increasing pitch by 4° restores mass flow rate to within 2% of the sea-level equivalent, maintaining the required condenser heat
rejection rate. This adjustment is made at the TMI factory and verified on the performance test rig; field adjustment is not permitted
and the blade pitch is marked with a paint witness mark on assembly.
(cid:127) Low-pressure cutout altitude correction: R-410A suction pressure at Denver is approximately 14 kPa lower than sea-level for
equivalent evaporating temperature, due to the reduced atmospheric back-pressure on the suction side. Standard low-pressure
cutout set-points would trigger nuisance trips at Denver without correction. The BACnet controller stores the altitude-corrected
suction pressure thresholds and applies them automatically; no field adjustment to refrigerant charge is required.
(cid:127) Phase 2 expansion provision: the pre-drilled structural flange adds 420 CAD to this project but eliminates the need to remove and
re-fabricate the condenser frame when the second unit is ordered. Phase 2 unit lead time will be standard (9 to 10 weeks) as no
expedite programme will be in place.
(cid:127) Roof structural loading: 590 kg shipping weight. Customer's structural engineer confirmed slab capacity at 1,200 kg/m2 design load;
unit footprint produces 680 kg/m2 — within capacity with margin.
Critical design decision: The critical design decision on this project was correcting all performance parameters — compressor
volumetric efficiency, condenser fan blade pitch, and low-pressure cutout set-points — for Denver's 1,609 m elevation and 0.833
sea-level air density ratio, rather than applying a generic altitude de-rating factor, ensuring the 95 kW capacity guarantee is met
precisely at site conditions without over-specifying the equipment.
